A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

About

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in India's missile and space programs, he was a key figure in the development of the country's nuclear capabilities. Dr. Kalam was widely respected for his humility, dedication to education, and inspirational vision for India's youth. His legacy continues to inspire millions across the world.

Biographies

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), contributing to India's space and missile programs.
  • Played a leading role in the development of ballistic missiles and launch vehicle technology, earning the nickname "Missile Man of India."
  • Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the DRDO.
  • Became the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
  • Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds."
  • Received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
  • Passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving a lasting legacy as a scientist, leader, and inspiration to millions.
